## Evidence Quality Assessment

### Strong Evidence Areas

**Financial Metrics and Calculations:**
- **Evidence Quality:** Strong
- **Rationale:** Well-established industry standards with decades of usage
- **Key Standards:**
  - FFO/AFFO: Defined by NAREIT, universally accepted for REITs
  - Leverage ratios: Consistent across rating agencies
  - Coverage metrics: Standard debt service and interest coverage formulas

**Credit Rating Methodologies:**
- **Evidence Quality:** Strong
- **Rationale:** Published frameworks from major rating agencies (Moody's, S&P, Fitch)
- **Documentation:** Detailed scorecards with factor weights and rating grids
- **Validation:** Decades of rating performance data

**REIT Regulatory Framework:**
- **Evidence Quality:** Strong
- **Rationale:** U.S. REIT rules codified in tax law (IRC Section 856)
- **Requirements:** 90% distribution, asset composition, income source rules
- **Global Standards:** Similar REIT structures in Singapore, Australia, Europe

**Due Diligence Processes:**
- **Evidence Quality:** Strong
- **Rationale:** Industry best practices from CFA curriculum, regulatory guidance (FDIC, SEC)
- **Validation:** Widely adopted by institutional investors and lenders

### Moderate Evidence Areas

**Property-Type Specific Metrics:**
- **Evidence Quality:** Moderate
- **Rationale:** Benchmarks vary by market, property type, and vintage
- **Limitations:** Less standardized than core financial metrics
- **Examples:** Development margins, cap rates, lease-up timelines
- **Approach:** Use ranges and cite comparable transactions

**ESG Credit Impact Scoring:**
- **Evidence Quality:** Moderate
- **Rationale:** Evolving frameworks, less historical data than traditional credit metrics
- **Standards:** Moody's ESG Credit Impact Scores, S&P ESG Evaluations
- **Limitations:** Subjective assessments, changing methodologies
- **Approach:** Follow rating agency frameworks, acknowledge uncertainty

**Private Company Valuation:**
- **Evidence Quality:** Moderate
- **Rationale:** Limited transparency, infrequent transactions
- **Challenges:** Appraisal subjectivity, lack of market pricing
- **Approach:** Use public comparables with liquidity discount adjustments

**Developer Credit Analysis:**
- **Evidence Quality:** Moderate
- **Rationale:** Heterogeneous business models, project-specific risks
- **Variability:** Cycle-dependent, market-specific
- **Approach:** Detailed project-level analysis, stress testing

### Limited Evidence Areas

**Stress Scenario Outcomes:**
- **Evidence Quality:** Limited
- **Rationale:** No perfect historical parallels for future crises
- **Approach:** Use multiple scenarios, sensitivity analysis
- **Caveat:** Acknowledge inherent uncertainty

**Emerging Property Types:**
- **Evidence Quality:** Limited
- **Examples:** Life sciences, data centers (rapidly evolving)
- **Challenge:** Limited historical performance data
- **Approach:** Conservative assumptions, higher risk premiums

**Post-COVID Office Dynamics:**
- **Evidence Quality:** Limited
- **Rationale:** Structural shift still unfolding
- **Uncertainty:** Remote work permanence, space utilization trends
- **Approach:** Scenario-based analysis, ongoing monitoring

## Key Limitations and Caveats

### Inherent Limitations

1. **Forward-Looking Uncertainty:**
   - All projections are estimates based on assumptions
   - Real estate cycles are difficult to predict
   - Black swan events cannot be anticipated

2. **Data Quality Dependency:**
   - Analysis quality depends on issuer disclosure
   - Private companies have limited transparency
   - Appraisals are point-in-time estimates

3. **Market-Specific Variability:**
   - Real estate is inherently local
   - National/regional trends may not apply to specific markets
   - Micro-market dynamics require granular analysis

### Methodological Limitations

1. **Scorecard vs. Actual Rating:**
   - Scorecards provide starting point, not final answer
   - Qualitative factors and forward view may override metrics
   - Analyst judgment is critical

2. **Peer Comparison Challenges:**
   - Perfect comparables rarely exist
   - Property mix, geographic footprint differ
   - Accounting policy differences affect comparability

3. **Stress Testing:**
   - Scenarios are illustrative, not predictive
   - Correlation assumptions may break down in crises
   - Management actions in stress not fully modeled

### Areas Requiring Professional Judgment

1. **Asset Valuation:**
   - Cap rate selection requires market expertise
   - Appraisal quality assessment is subjective
   - Development proforma review needs deep experience

2. **Management Quality:**
   - Track record assessment requires pattern recognition
   - Related-party transaction evaluation needs context
   - Financial policy commitment assessment is qualitative

3. **Market Outlook:**
   - Supply/demand balance projections
   - Regulatory and political risk assessment
   - Economic scenario probability weighting
