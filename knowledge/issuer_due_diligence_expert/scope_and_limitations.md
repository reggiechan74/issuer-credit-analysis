# Scope and Limitations - Issuer Due Diligence Expert

## This Expert Can:

### Financial Analysis and Calculations

- **Calculate all standard REIT metrics:**
  - Funds From Operations (FFO) and Adjusted FFO (AFFO)
  - Net Asset Value (NAV) per share
  - Dividend payout ratios
  - Same-store NOI growth

- **Access real-time market data via OpenBB Platform:**
  - Live REIT stock prices and trading volumes
  - Current Treasury yields and benchmark rates
  - Corporate bond spreads by rating category
  - Economic indicators (GDP, CPI, unemployment, Fed Funds rate)
  - Peer company financial statements and metrics
  - Real-time valuation multiples (P/B, P/FFO, dividend yields)

- **Calculate credit metrics with Moody's adjustments:**
  - Debt / Gross Assets
  - Net Debt / EBITDA
  - EBITDA / Interest Expense
  - Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR)
  - Fixed Charge Coverage Ratio

- **Calculate operating metrics:**
  - Portfolio occupancy rates
  - Weighted Average Lease Expiry (WALE)
  - Rental reversions
  - Cap rates and yields

- **Calculate developer-specific metrics:**
  - Development yield on cost
  - Development profit margins
  - Loan-to-Cost (LTC) and Loan-to-Value (LTV)
  - Construction risk metrics

- **Perform debt reconciliations:**
  - Moody's-adjusted debt calculations
  - Moody's-adjusted EBITDA calculations
  - Treatment of hybrid/perpetual securities
  - Pro rata JV debt and EBITDA inclusion

### Credit Analysis

- **Apply rating scorecard methodologies:**
  - Assess issuers using Moody's 5-factor scorecard
  - Calculate scorecard-indicated outcomes
  - Explain variances between scorecard and actual rating assessment

- **Analyze credit strengths and challenges:**
  - Identify key credit drivers (positive and negative)
  - Assess business profile and competitive position
  - Evaluate financial policy and management discipline

- **Conduct liquidity analysis:**
  - Calculate sources and uses of liquidity
  - Assess debt maturity profile and refinancing risk
  - Evaluate covenant headroom and compliance risk

- **Perform ESG credit assessment:**
  - Assign ESG Credit Impact Scores
  - Evaluate environmental, social, and governance risks
  - Assess ESG factor materiality to credit quality

### Due Diligence

- **Specify information requirements:**
  - Identify required financial documents
  - Define operational data needs
  - Outline property-level information requirements

- **Guide verification processes:**
  - Recommend cross-checks and validations
  - Identify red flags in financial statements
  - Suggest due diligence priorities

- **Assess different issuer types:**
  - Public REITs
  - Real Estate Operating Companies (REOCs)
  - Private real estate operators and funds
  - Real estate developers
  - Hybrid developer + operator models

### Forecasting and Scenarios

- **Build financial forecasts:**
  - Project revenue based on same-store growth and portfolio changes
  - Forecast EBITDA using margin assumptions
  - Estimate interest expense and debt balances
  - Generate 2-3 year forward projections

- **Run sensitivity analyses:**
  - Stress test key assumptions (occupancy, rents, asset values, interest rates)
  - Calculate metric sensitivity to scenarios
  - Identify downgrade triggers and rating buffers

- **Conduct peer benchmarking:**
  - Select appropriate peer groups
  - Create comparative metrics tables
  - Calculate percentile rankings
  - Assess relative credit positioning

  - Create Moody's-style credit opinion reports

- **Produce Python-based calculations:**

  - Produce stacked bar charts (asset composition, debt maturity)

## Uncertainty and Limitations

### Inherent Analytical Uncertainty

**Forward-Looking Statements:**
- All projections are estimates based on assumptions
- Actual results will differ from forecasts
- Ranges should be used rather than point estimates
- Probability of scenarios is subjective

**Market Volatility:**
- Real estate values fluctuate
- Interest rates are unpredictable
- Economic conditions change rapidly
- Tenant demand is cyclical

**Information Asymmetry:**
- Issuers have more information than analysts
- Private companies provide limited disclosure
- Management incentives may bias presentations
- Verify information when possible, acknowledge when not

### Methodological Limitations

**Scorecard Rigidity:**
- Scorecards are mechanical starting points
- Cannot capture all relevant factors
- Weights may not fit all situations
- Judgment is required to override when appropriate

**Historical Relationships:**
- Past patterns may not predict future
- Structural changes can break correlations
- Unprecedented events (COVID-19) invalidate historical precedents

**Peer Comparability:**
- Perfect peers rarely exist
- Accounting differences affect comparability
- Business model nuances matter
- Context is critical for interpretation

### Data Quality Constraints

**Reliance on Issuer Data:**
- Analysis quality depends on input quality
- Errors in source data propagate
- Intentional misrepresentation is possible
- Verify when possible, disclose limitations when not

**Appraisal Subjectivity:**
- Property valuations are opinions
- Appraiser bias can exist
- Cap rate selection is subjective
- Stress test valuation assumptions

**Private Company Opacity:**
- Limited audited financials
- No market pricing for validation
- Infrequent valuations
- Apply conservatism and higher risk premiums
