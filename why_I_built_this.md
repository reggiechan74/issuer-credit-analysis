# Why I Built This

---

Let me tell you a story.

It's 3am. You're still at your desk. You've been working on this credit analysis for six hours.

Six. Hours.

And you're only halfway through.

Your eyes are burning from staring at PDFs. Your back hurts from hunching over your desk. You've copied so many numbers from one screen to another that your hand is cramping.

And somewhere around hour four, you realized something:

**This is insane.**

This is 2025. We have cars that drive themselves. We have phones that recognize your face. We have AI that can write poetry and paint pictures.

**And credit analysts are still copying numbers from PDFs. By hand. Like it's 1985.**

I sat there at 3am and thought: There has to be a better way.

---

## The Thing Nobody Talks About

Here's what they don't tell you when you become a credit analyst:

**You don't analyze credit. You copy numbers.**

You spend your entire day hunting through financial statements, finding data, copying it to Excel, checking it twice, then doing calculations that should be automatic.

By the time you're done, you're exhausted.

**And that's when they ask you to make the actual decision:** Is this credit good or bad?

Your brain is fried. You've been in the weeds for hours. You've lost perspective.

**This is backwards.**

You should spend your time *thinking*. Making judgments. Using your brain for what brains are actually good at.

Instead, you're a human copy machine.

---

## What They Tried (And Why It Doesn't Work)

"Just use AI!" they said. "Upload it to ChatGPT! Problem solved!"

So you try it.

And it fails. Or it gives you wrong numbers. Or it costs $150. Or it takes 20 minutes and you still don't trust it.

**This is not a solution. This is a different kind of broken.**

---

## The Moment Everything Changed

I was sitting there one day, staring at yet another REIT about to cut its distribution.

And I thought: *This should be automatic. Not 6 hours of manual work.*

**Why doesn't this just... work?**

And then it hit me.

Everyone's trying to solve the wrong problem. They're trying to make AI smarter, bigger, faster.

**What if we just made it simpler?**

What if we stopped trying to do everything at once? What if we broke it down into steps, like cooking a meal?

**What if we just let each piece do what it does best?**

---

## What I Built

I built a tool that runs automatically.

You give it two PDFs. Financial statements. Management discussion. Whatever you've got.

**You hit go. You get coffee. 30 minutes later, you have a complete credit analysis.**

Not a draft. Not a summary. **A complete, professional credit opinion.**

With all the metrics calculated. All the analysis done. Distribution cut prediction. Peer comparison. Scenario analysis. Everything.

**It just works.**

Every single time.

---

## The Part I'm Most Proud Of

There's a piece of this tool that predicts when REITs will cut their distributions.

12 months in advance.

I tested it on a REIT that was in trouble. Deep trouble. Burning cash. Barely surviving.

The old way of looking at it said: "Everything's fine. Risk is very low."

**My tool said: "This is a disaster waiting to happen. 67% chance they cut."**

**That's the difference between losing money and saving it.**

That's the difference between getting caught off guard and seeing it coming.

That's the difference between being a human spreadsheet and being an actual analyst.
