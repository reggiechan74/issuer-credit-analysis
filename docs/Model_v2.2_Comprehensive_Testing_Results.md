## Key Insights

### 1. Sustainable AFCF Methodology Identifies Hidden Risks

The key differentiator of model v2.2 is the use of **sustainable AFCF** which excludes non-recurring items like property dispositions. This reveals distribution cut risk that traditional metrics (AFFO payout) miss.

**Example - HR REIT:**
- AFFO Payout: 204.1% (obviously unsustainable)
- Total AFCF: $40M (positive due to $210M asset sales)
- Sustainable AFCF: $(170)M (negative, indicating true cash burn)
- **Model Prediction: 98.6% cut probability** ‚úÖ

**Example - RioCan REIT:**
- AFFO Payout: 71.2% (appears sustainable)
- Total AFCF: $155M (positive due to $200M asset sales)
- Sustainable AFCF: $(45)M (negative without asset sales)
- **Model Prediction: 48.5% cut probability** (High risk despite 71% payout)

### 2. Asset Sales Masking Operating Cash Burn

**8 out of 13 REITs** show significant gaps between total AFCF and sustainable AFCF:
- HR REIT: $210M gap (property dispositions)
- RioCan: $200M gap (asset recycling program)
- Dream Office: $185M gap (portfolio rationalization)
- NorthWest Healthcare: $123M gap (dispositions)

**Implication:** These REITs rely on asset sales to generate positive cash flow. Sustainable AFCF reveals the underlying cash burn.

### 3. Distribution Payout Ratios Alone Are Misleading

**InterRent REIT Example:**
- AFFO Payout: 69.8% (appears sustainable)
- AFCF Sustainable: $(58)M (negative)
- Self-Funding Ratio: -0.52x
- **Model Prediction: 54.1% cut probability**

Traditional AFFO payout analysis would classify InterRent as "low risk" (69.8% < 85% threshold). However, sustainable AFCF reveals development capex of $(75.6)M is consuming all operating cash flow, creating distribution cut risk if development slows.
