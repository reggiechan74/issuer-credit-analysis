## Executive Summary

**Scorecard-Indicated Rating:** Ba2 / Ba3 (analysis-derived)

Allied Properties Real Estate Investment Trust faces severe financial stress as of Q3 2025, with credit metrics indicating a **Ba3/B1** rating positioning in the non-investment grade category. The REIT's credit profile is characterized by unsustainable distribution coverage, elevated leverage, and significant refinancing risk that collectively point to imminent capital structure adjustment.

The most critical vulnerability is distribution sustainability. With an AFFO payout ratio of 166.7% (issuer-reported), Allied is distributing $1.67 for every dollar of adjusted cash flow generated, a fundamentally unsustainable position. Our distribution cut prediction model assigns a **59.9% probability** of an imminent distribution cut, classifying Allied as "High Risk." This assessment is corroborated by recent distribution history‚ÄîAllied already cut its distribution by 52.4% in January 2024, and current payout ratios indicate minimal restoration of financial health since that action. The recovery level of only 38.6% from pre-cut levels signals ongoing distress rather than stabilization.

Leverage metrics compound the distribution stress. At 83.6% debt-to-assets and 82.95% net debt ratio, Allied operates with minimal equity cushion. The debt burden is particularly concerning given net debt reaching 12.3x annualized adjusted EBITDA‚Äîwell above the 10.0x target management has committed to achieving. Interest coverage of 2.44x NOI-to-interest provides limited buffer, especially considering the REIT recorded a $315.8 million IFRS net loss for the nine-month period driven by $394.1 million in unrealized fair value losses on investment properties. These losses reflect market recognition of asset value impairment in the challenged urban office sector.

Market indicators reinforce the credit stress diagnosis. Allied's unit price of $15.26 represents a 31.5% decline from its 52-week peak, with the security exhibiting high volatility (39.9% annualized over 90 days) and negative momentum (-11.8% over three months). Our market risk score of 75/100 ("Very High") indicates capital markets perceive significant distress. The unit price trades at a 60% discount to stated NAV per unit ($38.05), suggesting investors expect further asset writedowns or equity dilution.

However, Allied retains meaningful strengths that prevent a deeper rating assessment. The REIT maintains $803 million in available liquidity ($903 million including accordion features), providing essential runway to execute strategic deleveraging. The portfolio consists of 9.5 million square feet of urban workspace concentrated in Canada's major markets, with 87.4% leased occupancy demonstrating resilient tenant demand despite sectoral headwinds. Management has articulated a clear deleveraging strategy centered on asset monetization, including the pending sale of the 150 West Georgia loan receivable expected to close in H1 2026. The easing macro interest rate environment (Bank of Canada policy rate declining 125 bps to 2.75%) provides tailwinds for refinancing the $2.27 billion in debt maturing 2026-2028.

**Scorecard-Indicated Rating: Ba3/B1** reflecting elevated credit risk with significant downside potential absent successful execution of deleveraging and distribution adjustment strategies. The rating could deteriorate to B2/B3 if management delays necessary capital structure adjustments or fails to execute planned asset sales. Conversely, aggressive deleveraging combined with distribution reset could support Ba2 positioning within 12-18 months.

## Rating Outlook

**Outlook: Stable**

**Rationale:**

**Outlook:** Negative (12-18 months)

**Rationale:**

Allied Properties' credit outlook is **Negative**, reflecting our assessment that credit metrics are more likely to deteriorate than improve over the next 12-18 months absent aggressive management intervention. The Negative outlook is driven by three primary factors: (1) the mathematical impossibility of sustaining current distribution levels given 166.7% AFFO payout ratios, (2) execution risk around $2.27 billion in debt refinancing maturing 2026-2028, and (3) ongoing property value deterioration in the urban office sector as reflected in $394 million fair value losses year-to-date.

Our distribution cut prediction model assigns a **59.9% probability** of an imminent distribution cut, the single most important forward-looking indicator of credit stress. The model's top predictive features highlight fundamental cash flow shortfalls: low ACFO (calculated), constrained self-funding ratio, and negative monthly burn rate. These metrics indicate Allied is consuming rather than generating discretionary cash flow, a condition that cannot persist indefinitely without balance sheet repair.

The 12.3x net debt-to-EBITDA ratio‚Äîsignificantly above management's stated <10x target‚Äîdemonstrates limited progress on deleveraging since the January 2024 distribution cut. While management articulates a clear strategy (asset monetization, debt reduction), execution timelines have slipped. The critical 150 West Georgia loan receivable monetization, originally expected by year-end 2025, now targets H1 2026 closing. Similar delays in non-core asset sales extend the period of elevated financial risk.

Market-based indicators reinforce credit stress. Allied's unit price has declined 31.5% from its 52-week peak to $15.26, with high volatility (61.5% annualized over 30 days, 39.9% over 90 days) and negative momentum (-11.8% over 3 months). The market risk score of 75/100 ("Very High") reflects investor perception of significant downside. The 60% discount between market price ($15.26) and stated NAV per unit ($38.05) suggests either substantial future asset writedowns or dilutive equity issuance are anticipated.

The Negative outlook does not imply default risk is imminent. Allied maintains $803 million in available liquidity providing adequate runway through 2026. However, the outlook reflects our view that credit quality is more likely to weaken than strengthen, either through:
1. **Delayed distribution cut:** If management postpones inevitable distribution reduction, cash burn accelerates and refinancing capacity diminishes
2. **Asset sales below book value:** Forced sales at distressed pricing to meet covenant requirements or liquidity needs
3. **Refinancing at elevated spreads:** Credit market repricing of Allied debt given sector stress and credit deterioration

A stabilization of the outlook to Stable would require concrete evidence of: (1) distribution reduction to sustainable 80-90% AFFO payout, (2) successful asset monetization at or near book value, (3) meaningful deleveraging toward <10x net debt/EBITDA, and (4) occupancy and NOI stabilization.

Allied announces distribution cut of 25-35% within Q4 2025 or Q1 2026, reducing monthly distribution from $0.1216/unit to $0.08-0.09/unit range. This adjustment brings AFFO payout ratio to sustainable 85-95% range, aligning with sector norms for non-investment grade REITs. Concurrently, Allied executes 150 West Georgia loan monetization in Q1/Q2 2026 generating $250-300 million proceeds (net) applied to debt reduction. Additional non-core asset sales total $200-250 million in 2026, bringing total debt reduction to $450-550 million.

**What is Burn Rate:**
Burn rate measures the forward-looking speed at which a REIT depletes cash reserves based on current run rate. This is a **liquidity stress test** assuming NO future access to capital markets. A REIT can have *positive AFCF* but still burn cash if free cash flow is insufficient to cover mandatory obligations (debt service + distributions).

**Formula:**
```
Mandatory Obligations = Debt Service + Distributions (period amounts)
Period Deficit = AFCF - Mandatory Obligations (when AFCF < Obligations)
Monthly Burn Rate = Period Deficit / Number of Months in Period
```

**Note:** New financing is NOT subtracted - burn rate assumes no future capital raises.

```

### Burn Rate and Cash Runway Metrics

**Burn Rate Applicability:**
```
Burn Rate applies when: AFCF < Total Financing Obligations

Where:
  Total Financing Obligations = Debt Service + Distributions

**Monthly Burn Rate:**
```
Period Deficit = AFCF - Mandatory Obligations (when AFCF < Obligations)
Monthly Burn Rate = Period Deficit / Number of Months in Period

Where:
  Mandatory Obligations = Total Debt Service + Total Distributions
  New financing is NOT subtracted (assumes no future capital raises)
```

**Self-Funding Ratio (Burn Rate Context):**
```
Self-Funding Ratio = AFCF / Mandatory Obligations

  0.8-1.0x = Low burn rate (nearly self-funding)
  0.5-0.8x = Moderate burn rate (moderate capital markets reliance)
  < 0.5x = High burn rate (high capital markets reliance)
```

**Cash Runway:**
```
Available Cash = Cash + Marketable Securities - Restricted Cash

Cash Runway (months) = Available Cash / |Monthly Burn Rate|

Extended Runway = (Available Cash + Undrawn Facilities) / |Monthly Burn Rate|

Where:
  Undrawn Facilities = Remaining credit facility capacity
  |Monthly Burn Rate| = Absolute value of monthly burn rate
```

**Liquidity Risk Assessment:**
```
Risk Level based on Cash Runway:
  < 6 months    = CRITICAL (Score 4) - Immediate financing required
  6-12 months   = HIGH (Score 3) - Near-term capital raise needed
  12-24 months  = MODERATE (Score 2) - Plan financing within 12 months
  > 24 months   = LOW (Score 1) - Adequate liquidity runway
```

**Sustainable Burn Rate:**
```
Target Runway = 24 months (default)
Sustainable Monthly Burn = Available Cash / Target Runway

Excess Burn = Actual Monthly Burn - Sustainable Monthly Burn

Status:
  Excess Burn < 0 = Below sustainable (cash cushion)
  Excess Burn > 0 = Above sustainable (corrective action needed)
```
