## Executive Summary

Dream Industrial Real Estate Investment Trust (Dream Industrial REIT or DIR) demonstrates strong credit fundamentals anchored by investment-grade leverage metrics, excellent portfolio quality, and a conservative financial policy. With Debt/Gross Assets of 28.4% and NOI/Interest Coverage of 4.4x, DIR positions firmly in the 'A' rating category based on quantitative metrics. The REIT's 72.9 million square foot industrial portfolio across 338 properties exhibits exceptional occupancy of 96.0%, benefiting from robust structural demand for logistics and warehouse space. The conservative FFO payout ratio of 68.6% provides meaningful financial flexibility for growth and deleveraging. Credit quality is supported by DIR's scale ($8.3 billion gross assets), access to both Canadian and U.S. capital markets, and disciplined capital allocation. The primary challenge is the ongoing transition in industrial real estate fundamentals following the post-pandemic supply surge, though DIR's high occupancy suggests minimal immediate pressure.

## Credit Strengths

**1. Conservative Leverage Profile (Debt/Assets: 28.4%)**
- Debt to Gross Assets of 28.4% positions DIR at the conservative end of the 'A' rating category (25-35% range)
- Net Debt ratio of 27.85% demonstrates strong liquidity position with $42.6M cash on balance sheet
- Total debt of $2.35 billion against $8.27 billion gross assets provides substantial cushion for asset value volatility
- Leverage metrics significantly better than 'Baa' threshold (35-45%), providing multi-notch buffer

**2. Robust Debt Servicing Capacity (4.4x NOI Coverage)**
- NOI/Interest coverage of 4.4x sits comfortably in the 'A' range (4.0-6.0x)
- Annualized interest expense of $169.5M against $186.4M annualized NOI (6-month basis) demonstrates strong debt servicing

**3. Exceptional Portfolio Occupancy and Quality**
- 96.0% occupancy rate significantly exceeds typical industrial REIT averages (91-93%)
- 338-property portfolio with 72.9M SF provides excellent tenant and geographic diversification
- Industrial property type benefits from structural tailwinds: e-commerce, supply chain modernization, nearshoring

**5. Meaningful Scale and Diversification**

- 338 properties across multiple markets mitigates single-asset concentration risk
- 72.9M SF portfolio provides operating leverage and economies of scale in property management

## Credit Challenges

**1. AFFO Metric Not Disclosed**
- Phase 3 metrics show AFFO of $0 and AFFO payout ratio of 0.0%, indicating metric was not extracted or calculated
- AFFO (Adjusted Funds From Operations) provides more conservative view of cash generation after maintenance capex
- Lack of AFFO visibility creates uncertainty around true distributable cash flow and sustainability of 68.6% FFO payout
- Typical AFFO/FFO ratios range 85-95%, suggesting actual payout ratio may be 72-81% range if AFFO assumed at 90% of FFO

**2. Limited Same-Property NOI Growth Visibility**
- Same-property NOI growth reported as 0% in Phase 3 metrics
- Zero growth may reflect: (1) actual stagnation, (2) data extraction limitation, or (3) portfolio repositioning activities
- Industrial sector typically exhibits 2-5% same-property NOI growth in stable markets
- Inability to assess organic growth trajectory limits confidence in future coverage ratio stability

**3. Industrial Market Normalization Risks**
- Industrial sector experiencing supply-side normalization after 2020-2022 development boom
- Elevated deliveries in key markets may pressure rental rate growth and occupancy in 2025-2026
- While DIR's 96% occupancy suggests strong positioning, market-wide trends could eventually impact portfolio
- Lease expiry management will be critical as tenants gained negotiating leverage in some markets

**4. Scale Positioning (Borderline 'A' Category)**
- Gross assets of $8.27B fall below the $10-20B threshold for clear 'A' scale rating
- Positions DIR in upper 'Baa' / lower 'A' range from pure size perspective (Factor 1: 5% weighting)
- Smaller scale versus U.S. industrial REIT leaders (Prologis ~$160B, Duke Realty ~$25B pre-merger) may limit:
  - Negotiating leverage with institutional tenants
  - Access to lowest-cost capital
  - Development pipeline scale economics

## Rating Outlook

**STABLE**

The Stable outlook reflects our expectation that Dream Industrial REIT will maintain leverage in the 27-30% Debt/Gross Assets range and NOI coverage above 4.0x over the next 12-18 months. The outlook incorporates the following assumptions:

**Supporting Factors:**
- **Portfolio resilience:** 96% occupancy expected to remain above 94% given strong tenant retention and limited large-block expirations
- **Leverage stability:** Asset values and NOI expected to remain stable, supporting Debt/Assets in 28-30% range
- **Distribution sustainability:** Conservative 68.6% FFO payout provides cushion for modest FFO volatility
- **Capital allocation discipline:** Management track record suggests bias toward balance sheet preservation over aggressive growth

**Monitoring Factors:**
- Industrial market supply/demand dynamics in core Canadian and U.S. markets
- Lease renewal spreads and occupancy trends through 2025-2026
- Deployment of retained cash flow (~$94M annualized) toward debt reduction vs. acquisitions
- Interest rate environment and refinancing costs for maturing debt

The Stable outlook could shift to **Positive** if DIR demonstrates sustained same-property NOI growth of 3%+ while maintaining leverage below 27%, or executes accretive portfolio repositioning that enhances asset quality. Conversely, the outlook could shift to **Negative** if occupancy declines below 93%, leverage exceeds 32%, or coverage falls below 3.8x due to market deterioration or aggressive capital deployment.

## Upgrade Factors

An upgrade to **'A' or 'A+'** would require DIR to demonstrate the following improvements over a sustained 12-18 month period:

**Quantitative Thresholds:**
1. **Debt/Gross Assets sustained below 27%** (currently 28.4%)
   - Requires approximately $115M of debt reduction or $435M of asset growth at constant leverage
   - Path: Apply retained FFO (~$94M annualized) to debt paydown, or execute asset sales above book value

2. **NOI/Interest Coverage sustained above 5.0x** (currently 4.4x)
   - Requires NOI growth of 14% or interest expense reduction of 12%
   - Path: Same-property NOI growth of 3-4% annually, combined with refinancing at lower rates

3. **Scale expansion to $10B+ gross assets**
   - Requires $1.7B of accretive acquisitions or $1.7B of asset appreciation
   - Must be achieved without increasing leverage above 30%

**Qualitative Improvements:**
1. **Geographic/tenant diversification enhancement**
   - Expand presence in high-growth U.S. logistics corridors (Sunbelt, Inland Empire, etc.)
   - Secure additional investment-grade tenant relationships (currently quality unclear from metrics)

2. **Development pipeline establishment**
   - Create development program generating 8-10% yields on cost
   - Demonstrate ability to pre-lease development projects above 70% prior to completion

3. **ESG leadership positioning**
   - Achieve carbon neutrality targets
   - Secure green building certifications (LEED, BREEAM) for majority of portfolio
   - Establish green financing framework with sustainability-linked pricing

**Timeframe:** 18-24 months of sustained performance at these thresholds

**Scenarios Leading to Downgrade:**

1. **Industrial Market Downturn (Probability: 15-20%)**
   - Economic recession drives tenant bankruptcies and negative absorption
   - Occupancy declines to 88-90% range over 12-18 months
   - Rental rate compression of 5-10% on renewals and new leases
   - **Pro forma impact:** Debt/Assets rises to 30-32% (asset value decline), coverage falls to 3.2-3.5x

2. **Aggressive Debt-Funded Acquisition Program (Probability: 10-15%)**
   - Management pursues $1B+ acquisition program funded 60%+ with debt
   - Acquisitions fail to generate expected NOI yields due to integration challenges
   - **Pro forma impact:** Debt/Assets rises to 36-38%, coverage falls to 3.8-4.0x

3. **Refinancing Risk Materialization (Probability: 5-10%)**
   - $500M+ debt maturity block refinanced at 150-200 bps higher rates
   - Interest expense increases 20-25% ($34M annually)
   - **Pro forma impact:** Coverage falls to 3.4-3.6x while leverage remains stable

4. **Distribution Policy Shift (Probability: 5%)**
   - Management increases FFO payout to 90%+ to compete with high-yield REIT peers
   - Reduces financial flexibility and retained capital for deleveraging
   - **Pro forma impact:** Limited debt reduction capacity, slower path to leverage improvement

**Early Warning Indicators:**
- Same-property NOI growth turns negative for 2+ consecutive quarters
- Occupancy declines below 93% for 6+ months
- Debt/Assets trending above 31% for 2+ quarters
- Management commentary shifts toward "growth at all costs" messaging
- FFO payout ratio increases above 80%

## Appendix A: Calculation Methodologies

### Leverage Metrics

**Debt / Gross Assets:**
```
Debt / Gross Assets = Total Debt / Gross Book Value

  Total Debt = Mortgages (current + non-current) + Credit Facilities + Senior Unsecured Debentures

```
Net Debt Ratio = (Total Debt - Cash) / Gross Book Value
