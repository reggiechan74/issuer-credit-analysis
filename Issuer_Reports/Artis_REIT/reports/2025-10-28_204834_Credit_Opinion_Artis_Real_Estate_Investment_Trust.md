## Rating Outlook

**Outlook: Stable**

**Rationale:**

**Outlook:** Negative (12-18 months)

**Justification:**

The **Negative outlook** reflects a **high probability (>50%) of downgrade** to B or lower within the next 12-18 months absent immediate and successful remedial action. Four critical scenarios drive the outlook:

**Base Case (40% probability) ‚Äì Downgrade to B:**
- Distribution suspension/cut implemented within 60 days, conserving $18M annually
- Asset sales generate $50-100M in liquidity at 10-20% discount to book value
- Credit facility renewals secured but at higher spreads (covenant restrictions tightened)
- Cash runway extended to 18-24 months, allowing controlled deleveraging
- **Outcome:** Downgrade to B as distribution cut signals financial distress, but survival secured

**Upside Case (25% probability) ‚Äì Rating Affirmed at B+:**
- Major asset sale or portfolio sale generates $150-200M at or near book value
- Strategic transaction (merger, take-private) provides permanent capital solution
- Distribution policy reset to sustainable level (60-70% AFFO payout) with credible long-term plan
- Debt reduced by 20-30%, improving coverage ratios and runway
- **Outcome:** B+ rating affirmed with Stable outlook if liquidity crisis fully resolved

**Downside Case (30% probability) ‚Äì Downgrade to B- or Caa:**
- Distribution maintained or cut insufficient (<50% reduction)
- Asset sales fail to materialize or realize 30%+ discounts
- Credit facility renewal blocked or requires onerous terms (higher fees, cash sweeps, equity cure provisions)
- Cash runway exhausted within 6-9 months, covenant breach imminent
- **Outcome:** Downgrade to B- (financial distress) or Caa (default risk) as restructuring scenarios emerge

**Distressed Case (5% probability) ‚Äì Default/Restructuring:**
- Unable to access capital markets (debt or equity)
- Covenant breach triggers acceleration or payment default
- Forced liquidation at distressed prices
- **Outcome:** Rating withdrawn or downgraded to D (default)

**Key Monitoring Metrics:**
- Monthly cash position and burn rate trend
- Distribution policy announcements (suspension, reduction, or maintenance)
- Asset sale execution (pricing, timing, quantum)
- Credit facility renewal status (availability, pricing, covenants)
- Covenant compliance headroom (debt/assets, interest coverage)

**Rating Upgrade Factors (12-18 months):**
- Cash runway extended to >24 months through asset sales and distribution cut
- Self-funding ratio improves to >0.50x (can cover 50%+ of obligations from AFCF)
- Debt reduced to <38% of assets with interest coverage >2.0x
- Distribution policy reset to sustainable level (<90% AFFO payout)
- Market confidence restored (unit price recovery >20%, positive momentum)
- **Potential upgrade to BB- if liquidity crisis fully resolved and deleveraging path credible**

**Rating Downgrade Factors (6-12 months):**
- Cash runway falls below 6 months without credible financing plan
- Distribution maintained at current unsustainable level beyond Q3 2025
- Asset sales fail to execute or realize >20% discounts
- Covenant breach or technical default event
- Self-funding ratio deteriorates further (more negative AFCF)
- Market risk score increases >65/100 (severe distress pricing)
- **Immediate downgrade to B- or Caa if any of above materialize**

#### 4.3 Burn Rate and Cash Runway Analysis

**Applicability:** Yes

**What is Burn Rate:**
Burn rate measures the forward-looking speed at which a REIT depletes cash reserves based on current run rate. This is a **liquidity stress test** assuming NO future access to capital markets. A REIT can have *positive AFCF* but still burn cash if free cash flow is insufficient to cover mandatory obligations (debt service + distributions).

**Formula:**
```
Mandatory Obligations = Debt Service + Distributions (period amounts)
Period Deficit = AFCF - Mandatory Obligations (when AFCF < Obligations)
Monthly Burn Rate = Period Deficit / Number of Months in Period
```

**Note:** New financing is NOT subtracted - burn rate assumes no future capital raises.

### Burn Rate and Cash Runway Metrics

**Burn Rate Applicability:**
```
Burn Rate applies when: AFCF < Total Financing Obligations

Where:
  Total Financing Obligations = Debt Service + Distributions

Note: A REIT can have positive AFCF but still burn cash if free cash flow
      is insufficient to cover mandatory obligations.
```

**Monthly Burn Rate:**
```
Period Deficit = AFCF - Mandatory Obligations (when AFCF < Obligations)
Monthly Burn Rate = Period Deficit / Number of Months in Period

Where:
  Mandatory Obligations = Total Debt Service + Total Distributions
  New financing is NOT subtracted (assumes no future capital raises)
```

**Self-Funding Ratio (Burn Rate Context):**
```
Self-Funding Ratio = AFCF / Mandatory Obligations

Interpretation:
  ‚â• 1.0x = Self-funding (no burn rate - generates surplus)
  0.8-1.0x = Low burn rate (nearly self-funding)
  0.5-0.8x = Moderate burn rate (moderate capital markets reliance)
  < 0.5x = High burn rate (high capital markets reliance)
```

**Cash Runway:**
```
Available Cash = Cash + Marketable Securities - Restricted Cash

Cash Runway (months) = Available Cash / |Monthly Burn Rate|

Extended Runway = (Available Cash + Undrawn Facilities) / |Monthly Burn Rate|

Where:
  Undrawn Facilities = Remaining credit facility capacity
  |Monthly Burn Rate| = Absolute value of monthly burn rate
```

**Liquidity Risk Assessment:**
```
Risk Level based on Cash Runway:
  < 6 months    = CRITICAL (Score 4) - Immediate financing required
  6-12 months   = HIGH (Score 3) - Near-term capital raise needed
  12-24 months  = MODERATE (Score 2) - Plan financing within 12 months
  > 24 months   = LOW (Score 1) - Adequate liquidity runway
```

**Sustainable Burn Rate:**
```
Target Runway = 24 months (default)
Sustainable Monthly Burn = Available Cash / Target Runway

Excess Burn = Actual Monthly Burn - Sustainable Monthly Burn

Status:
  Excess Burn < 0 = Below sustainable (cash cushion)
  Excess Burn > 0 = Above sustainable (corrective action needed)
```
