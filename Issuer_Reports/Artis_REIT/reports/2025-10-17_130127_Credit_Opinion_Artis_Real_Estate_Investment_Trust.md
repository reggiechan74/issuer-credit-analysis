# Credit Opinion: Artis Real Estate Investment Trust

**Date:** October 17, 2025
**Reporting Period:** Unknown
**Currency:** CAD

---

## Executive Summary

Artis REIT's credit profile reflects a **Ba/BB credit quality** (high speculative grade), consistent with its DBRS BB (high) rating with stable trend. The trust demonstrates solid asset management capabilities with a diversified 9.7 million square foot portfolio across Canada (48%) and the US (52%), generating stable cash flows from industrial (34.4%), office (54.7%), and retail (10.9%) properties. However, the credit profile is constrained by **elevated leverage** (Debt/Assets 41.3%), **weak interest coverage** (NOI/Interest 1.8x, EBITDA/Interest 2.31x), and an **unsustainable distribution policy** (AFFO payout ratio 176.5%). Positively, management has demonstrated financial discipline through strategic asset sales ($51.7M in H1 2025), the $200M debenture repayment, and accretive unit buybacks (NCIB purchases at $7.42 vs. NAV of $12.98). The credit outlook is **stable** for the next 12-18 months, predicated on continued deleveraging through asset sales, maintaining portfolio occupancy above 87%, and prudent capital allocation.

**Scorecard-Indicated Rating:** Ba2/BB
**Key Rating Drivers:** Leverage position, interest coverage, distribution sustainability, deleveraging trajectory
**Primary Risk:** Refinancing risk from fully drawn revolving credit facility and limited covenant headroom

## Credit Strengths

### 3.1 **Solid Asset Management Execution with Positive Momentum**
Artis demonstrates effective portfolio management, delivering 2.3% same-property NOI growth over the 6-month period ending June 30, 2025. The weighted-average renewal increase of 3.6% in Q2 2025 indicates positive rental reversions and favorable negotiations with tenants. Portfolio occupancy of 87.8% (89.0% including commitments) reflects stable operations, though below best-in-class levels.

### 3.2 **Geographic Diversification Reduces Single-Market Risk**
The portfolio's 48% Canada / 52% US split (by GLA) provides meaningful geographic diversification and mitigates country-specific regulatory or economic risks. This binational footprint offers optionality for capital allocation and reduces correlation to any single real estate market cycle.

### 3.3 **Demonstrated Financial Discipline and Deleveraging Progress**
Management's recent actions signal improved financial stewardship:
- **Asset recycling:** Sold 5 properties for $51.7M proceeds in H1 2025, targeting non-core or lower-yielding assets
- **Debt reduction:** Repaid $200M senior unsecured debentures in Q2 2025, reducing fixed charges
- **Accretive capital allocation:** NCIB purchases of 3.8M units at $7.42 average price vs. NAV of $12.98 represents 43% discount to intrinsic value, creating meaningful value per remaining unit

### 3.4 **Meaningful Scale with $2.6B Asset Base**
Total assets of $2.6B and investment properties of $2.0B provide sufficient scale to:
- Access institutional capital markets
- Spread corporate overhead across a large asset base
- Negotiate favorable financing terms
- Attract quality tenants

### 3.5 **Positive Rental Momentum in Challenging Office Market**
Despite structural headwinds facing office (54.7% of GLA), Artis achieved 3.6% weighted-average renewal increases in Q2 2025, indicating tenant retention and pricing power in select submarkets. This suggests quality of specific assets within the office portfolio.

### 3.6 **Strategic Vision Aligned with Creditor Interests**
Management's stated vision emphasizes "best-in-class real estate asset management platform focused on value investing" and strategy prioritizes "strengthen balance sheet" alongside "drive organic growth." This alignment with credit quality improvement is positive for bondholder protection.

## Credit Challenges

### 4.1 **Elevated Leverage with Limited Deleveraging Runway**
Debt/Gross Assets of 41.3% and Net Debt ratio of 40.7% position Artis at the aggressive end of the Ba rating spectrum. With modest same-property NOI growth (~2.3% run-rate) and significant interest expense ($68.7M annualized), organic deleveraging will be slow absent accelerated asset sales. At current trajectory, meaningful leverage improvement (targeting <38% for Baa threshold) would require 3-4 years or substantial equity issuance.

### 4.2 **Weak Interest Coverage Leaves Minimal Cushion**
NOI/Interest coverage of 1.8x and EBITDA/Interest coverage of 2.31x provide limited margin for error. Both metrics sit at the lower end of Ba range:
- **Ba benchmark:** 2.0-3.25x EBITDA/Interest
- **Artis position:** 2.31x (only 8 bps above minimum threshold)
- **Risk:** Any portfolio occupancy decline (100 bps = ~$1M NOI = -0.015x coverage) or interest rate increase would quickly pressure coverage below 2.0x, triggering potential covenant concerns or downgrade risk

### 4.3 **Unsustainable Distribution Policy**
The AFFO payout ratio of 176.5% is fundamentally unsustainable and requires immediate attention:
- **AFFO per unit:** $0.34
- **Distributions per unit:** $0.60
- **Shortfall:** $0.26 per unit (76.5% deficit)
- **Implication:** Distributions currently funded by asset sales, debt proceeds, or balance sheet erosion rather than operating cash flow

While the FFO payout ratio of 88.2% appears marginally sustainable, AFFO is the superior metric as it accounts for maintenance capital expenditures. A distribution cut to $0.30-0.35 per unit would improve AFFO coverage to ~88-103%, which would be prudent from a credit perspective.

### 4.4 **Significant Refinancing Risk from Fully Drawn Revolver**
**Critical liquidity concern:** The revolving credit facility is fully drawn at $437.6M, representing:
- 40.6% of total debt ($1,079M)
- 16.8% of gross assets
- Cash of only $16.6M provides minimal buffer

This creates meaningful refinancing risk:
- **Maturity risk:** Facility maturity date not disclosed but typical revolvers are 3-5 year terms
- **Covenant risk:** No disclosed headroom on financial maintenance covenants
- **Market access risk:** Refinancing depends on maintaining credit metrics and capital market conditions
- **Flexibility constraint:** No undrawn capacity for acquisitions, development, or working capital needs

### 4.5 **Elevated Office Exposure During Structural Transition**
Office properties comprise 54.7% of total GLA, exposing Artis to secular headwinds:
- Post-pandemic office utilization pressures
- Remote/hybrid work reducing space needs per employee
- Tenant flight to quality (potential negative reversion for secondary assets)
- Rising capital expenditure requirements for ESG/amenity upgrades

While Artis has achieved positive rental reversions (+3.6% Q2 2025), the magnitude of renewal risk over next 3-5 years creates material downside scenario potential.

### 4.6 **Limited Financial Flexibility for Growth**
The combination of elevated leverage (41.3%), fully drawn revolver, and high distribution burden constrains strategic optionality:
- Minimal capacity for acquisitions without equity issuance
- Limited ability to fund value-add capex or developments
- Constrained response to unexpected tenant departures or property-specific issues
- Forced seller status if liquidity deteriorates
